# Getting Corbett Claims found on Google — owner instructions

**For:** Ronnie & Annie Corbett
**Prepared:** July 2026
**Time to complete:** ~1 hour total, spread over a few days

---

## The short version

Your website (`corbettclaims.com`) is **built correctly for search**. The page
title, description, business details, service list, hours, and the "structured
data" that Google reads are all already in place and accurate. There is
**nothing wrong with the site itself.**

The reason it doesn't show up when you search "corbett claims" is simpler: the
site is **new, and Google hasn't added it to its index yet.** A brand-new
website is invisible until Google discovers and files it away — that can take
weeks on its own. The steps below tell Google "we're here, come look now" and
build the local-business signals that make you show up for the *right* Corbett.

Do them in order. Step 1 is the one that actually gets you into Google.

---

## Step 1 — Tell Google the site exists (Google Search Console) ★ most important

This is the single step that gets `corbettclaims.com` into Google's index.

1. Go to **https://search.google.com/search-console** and sign in with a Google
   account (a Gmail account is fine — ideally one you'll keep for the business).
2. Click **Add property** → choose the **URL prefix** option → enter
   `https://www.corbettclaims.com/` → **Continue**.
3. Google will ask you to **verify you own the site**. The easiest method is
   usually **"HTML tag"** — it gives you a short line of code to add to the
   website. **Send that line to whoever manages the website** (or reply to this
   and it gets added in a couple minutes), then click **Verify**.
4. Once verified, in the left menu open **Sitemaps**, and in the "Add a new
   sitemap" box type `sitemap.xml` → **Submit**. (Your sitemap already exists at
   `https://www.corbettclaims.com/sitemap.xml`.)
5. Still in Search Console, use the **URL inspection** bar at the very top, paste
   `https://www.corbettclaims.com/`, press Enter, then click **Request
   Indexing**.

**What to expect:** after this, the site typically shows up in Google within a
few days to about two weeks (instead of waiting a month or more). Search Console
will also *tell you* if Google hits any problem crawling the site — so it's your
early-warning system too.

**How to check progress:** search Google for `site:corbettclaims.com` (type it
exactly, including `site:`). Today that shows nothing — your pages are not indexed
yet. Once it starts returning your own pages, you're in.

## Step 2 — Claim your Google Business Profile ★ big for a local business

For a local service like appraisals, this is what puts you on Google Maps and in
the little business box that appears above the regular results. It matters as
much as Step 1 for searches like "auto appraiser Baton Rouge."

1. Go to **https://www.google.com/business** and sign in with the **same Google
   account** you used in Step 1.
2. Search for "Corbett Claims" — if a listing already exists (older directory
   info sometimes creates one), **claim it**; if not, **create** it.
3. Fill in, matching the website exactly so Google trusts they're the same
   business:
   - **Name:** Corbett Claims
   - **Phone:** (225) 663-2217
   - **Website:** https://www.corbettclaims.com/
   - **Hours:** Mon–Fri 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM, closed Sat & Sun
   - **Service area:** set it as a *service-area business* covering Baton Rouge
     and the states you work (LA, TX, MS, AL, GA, FL, OH, IL, NV). You do **not**
     have to publish the PO Box as a street address if you'd rather not show one.
   - **Category:** "Appraiser" (add "Auto appraiser" if offered).
4. Google verifies by **postcard, phone, or email** — follow whichever it
   offers. Verification can take a few days (postcard is slowest).

Once live, ask a few clients you've worked with to leave a Google review — even
3–4 reviews meaningfully help you rank and win trust.

## Step 3 — Fix and connect your existing directory listings

There are already old listings floating around for "Corbett Claim Service" on
sites like **Manta, Alignable, Carwise, and MerchantCircle**, plus a LinkedIn
profile. Right now some of these outrank your real website, and a couple may
show **wrong or outdated info** (there's also an unrelated "Corbett Insurance"
in Pennsylvania and other Corbett businesses that muddy the search).

For each listing that is actually yours:

1. Claim it (each site has a "claim this business" or "is this your business?"
   link).
2. Correct the name, phone `(225) 663-2217`, hours, and service area so they
   **match the website exactly**.
3. Add your website link `https://www.corbettclaims.com/`. Each listing that
   links to your site is a small vote that helps the website rank.

Consistency is the point: Google trusts a business more when the name, phone, and
details are identical everywhere it appears.

## Step 4 — Be patient, then check back

After Steps 1–3:

- **Within ~2 weeks:** searching `site:corbettclaims.com` should return your
  pages, and searching **"Corbett Claims Baton Rouge"** should surface your site
  and/or your Google Business Profile near the top.
- **The plain phrase "corbett claims"** is *contested* — several real businesses
  use "Corbett" with insurance/claims. You'll likely win your own exact name once
  indexed, but the Google Business Profile (Step 2) and consistent listings
  (Step 3) are what let you stand out from the other Corbetts. This keeps
  improving over the following weeks.

If after ~3 weeks `site:corbettclaims.com` still returns nothing, open Search
Console (Step 1) — the **Pages** report there will say exactly why, and that's
the thing to send back for a fix.
